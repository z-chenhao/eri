# Code of Conduct

Eri welcomes people who want to improve a user-owned, local-first personal assistant. Participation in this repository must remain safe, constructive, and respectful.

## Expected behavior

- Discuss ideas and code on their merits; disagree without attacking the person.
- Make review feedback specific, actionable, and proportional to the risk.
- Respect privacy. Never post credentials, personal conversations, private logs, user datasets, or another person's identifying information.
- Disclose material limitations, safety concerns, licensing constraints, and AI assistance that affected a contribution.
- Stop and ask before testing against systems, accounts, or data you do not own or have explicit permission to use.

## Unacceptable behavior

Harassment, threats, discrimination, sexualized attention, doxxing, deliberate privacy violations, deceptive evidence, and attempts to pressure others into unsafe disclosure or execution are not accepted. Repeated disruption or bad-faith manipulation of the project's safety boundaries is also unacceptable.

## Reporting and enforcement

For ordinary disagreements, open a focused Issue or contact the maintainer through the relevant Pull Request. Do not publish sensitive conduct reports. For a private report, use the repository's **Security → Report a vulnerability** form and begin the title with `Conduct report:`. Only include the minimum information needed to investigate.

The maintainer may edit or remove content, close contributions, temporarily restrict participation, or permanently ban an account when necessary. Reports are handled as privately as the available GitHub tooling permits; conflicts of interest must be disclosed, and enforcement decisions should be documented without exposing the reporter or affected people.

This policy applies to repository Issues, Pull Requests, Discussions, review comments, and project-related communication in other spaces controlled by the Eri maintainers.
